01 — Foundation

What is music,
technically?

Before explaining what Prometheus does, it helps to understand what music actually consists of — because that's exactly what Prometheus extracts.

Any piece of recorded music is a raw audio waveform — millions of numbers representing air pressure over time. Hidden inside that waveform are structured musical objects: individual notes with pitches and durations, chords built from those notes, a rhythmic grid of beats and bars, and a tonal key that gives the whole piece its emotional character. Prometheus reads a raw audio file and outputs all of these as structured, actionable data.

🎵

Notes

A single sound with a specific pitch (how high or low) and duration. Middle C on a piano, a guitar string plucked at the 5th fret — these are notes. Prometheus detects every note played, including multiple simultaneous ones.

🎸

Chords

Three or more notes played together that create harmony. Em (E minor), Cmaj7, D7 — the building blocks of every song's emotional arc. Prometheus identifies them, names them, and places them in time.

🥁

Beats & Bars

The rhythmic pulse of music. A bar (or measure) groups beats — typically 4 per bar in pop music. Knowing "this chord lands on Bar 3, Beat 1" is what makes music synchronised and usable in production.

🔑

Key

The tonal centre of the song — the "home base" all the chords relate to. E minor, D major, A minor. Knowing the key is essential for transposing, remixing, or layering new instruments.

🎹

MIDI

Musical Instrument Digital Interface — a universal file format that encodes notes, timing, velocity, and instrument type. Every DAW (music production software) reads MIDI. It's the bridge between audio analysis and music production.

🏭

DAW

Digital Audio Workstation — software like Ableton, Logic Pro, FL Studio, or Cubase. Where music gets produced, mixed, and arranged. Prometheus outputs files that plug directly into any DAW without conversion.

03 — Rhythmic Grid

Every chord placed on
the exact beat

Knowing a chord's name is one thing. Knowing it lands on Bar 2, Beat 3 at 136 BPM is what makes it production-ready.

Prometheus runs librosa's beat tracker to compute the full rhythmic grid of the song — BPM, beats, and bars — then places every detected chord precisely on that grid. This is what "bars and beats sync" means: a guitarist, producer, or arranger can immediately see where each chord falls without listening to the song.

Transposition

Every detected chord can be shifted up or down any number of semitones — instantly changing the key of the entire song. A guitarist who plays in open E but needs the song in G? --transpose +3. A vocalist whose range sits a fifth higher? --transpose +7. The MIDI file shifts with it.
